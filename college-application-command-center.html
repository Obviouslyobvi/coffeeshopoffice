The Problem Parents Face

Your high school student is applying to 8-15 colleges. That means:

8-15 different application portals with different deadlines (Early Decision, Early Action, Regular Decision, Rolling Admissions)
Multiple essay requirements—Common App personal essay, supplemental essays for each school, sometimes 3-5 essays per application
Recommendation letters from teachers and counselors, each school with different requirements and submission deadlines
Financial aid chaos—FAFSA forms, CSS Profiles, scholarship deadlines, financial aid award comparisons
Test scores—SAT/ACT dates, score reporting to each school, test-optional decisions
Campus visits to organize, research forms to fill out, visit reflection notes to track

Without a system, deadlines slip. Requirements get forgotten. Financial aid decisions become impossible to compare. And you're left playing coordinator with your phone and 47 sticky notes.
