Inside the System: 9 Complete Worksheets

1. Getting Started Guide

A gentle overview that respects your pace. You'll learn how to use the system and why there's no "right" way to grieve.

2. Daily Grief Journal

Structured prompts for processing: "Today I feel...", "One memory I'm grateful for...", "What's hardest right now...", "One small thing I did for myself..."

3. Memory Preservation Worksheets

Pages to capture stories they told, traditions you shared, recipes they loved, favorite sayings, inside jokes—everything that kept them alive in your life.

4. Trigger & Anniversary Planner

Map the difficult dates and what triggers them. Build coping strategies for each. Reflect after each one passes.

5. Support Network Tracker

Organize therapist info, grief group details, and trusted contacts. Know who to call when you need crisis support, practical help, or just someone to listen.

6. New Normal Routine Builder

Loss changes your life. This helps you rebuild week by week. What needs to change? What do you want to keep? What small goals matter?

7. Self-Care Energy Budget

Track your energy levels and match them with activities that actually sustain you. Grief fatigue is real—this helps you honor that.

8. Milestone Marker Pages

Space to reflect on important moments: first birthday without them, first holiday, one-year anniversary. Includes self-compassion exercises.

9. Continuing Bonds Letters

Write letters to stay connected. Prompts include: "I wish I could tell you...", "You would be proud that...", "I still carry with me..."

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I use this?

Take what you need, leave what you don't. You can work through it all, or dip in as needed. Print it, use it digitally on a tablet, or share sections with a therapist. There's no right way.

How long is it?

9 complete worksheets with 20+ pages total. Print-friendly design. You can work through it in weeks or return to it over months and years.

Is this a substitute for therapy?

No. This workbook supports your grief process, but is not a substitute for professional mental health care. If you're in crisis or having thoughts of self-harm, please reach out to a mental health professional immediately.

When should I use this?

There's no "right" time. Some people use it immediately after loss. Others use it weeks, months, or years later. Grief doesn't follow a timeline.

What if I don't have a therapist?

The support network tracker helps you think through who you can reach out to. We've also included resources and suggestions for finding grief counselors and support groups if you need them.

Can I print this?

Absolutely. All PDFs are print-friendly. You can also use them on a tablet or computer. It's your choice.
